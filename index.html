Who I Am

Not a promise.
A system.

"You will always know what your union president is doing and you will have the tools to hold everyone accountable, including me."

Moses Nyambe is a Development Studies student at the University of Zambia Great East Road Campus currently serving as the National Think Tank Director and the Head of Research and Policy Committee for Make Zambia Great Movement and former Head Boy of Chibolya Secondary School.

I am not contesting out of ambition. I am because I sat where you are sitting trying to understand why I could not get accommodation, asking what criteria were being used, and receiving no answer. Not a wrong answer. No answer.

That silence is what I am running against.

Every candidate will promise you accommodation. Safety. Infrastructure. Jobs. Ask them how. I am offering something harder and more valuable: a system you can use to hold me, management, and this university accountable starting day one.

The Four Pillars

Real actions.
Honest scope.

Pillar 01 — Accommodation

Transparency Over False Promises

UNZA has 4,150 beds for over 30,000 students. No president can build a hostel. What I can do — and what no previous president has done is make allocation transparent and documented.

→ Publish bed-space allocation criteria within 30 days
→ Formal petition to VC, Dean of Students, Higher Education Authority
→ Log every corruption allegation — publicly
→ Student database of fair off-campus landlords and rental prices
Pillar 02 — Safety

Documentation Saves Lives

In March 2026, Emmanuel Bwalya — 18 years old — drowned in an open pit on this campus at 9pm. Management knew the sewer system was failing. There was no paper trail. That cannot happen again.

→ Student-run campus hazard reporting system accessible on any phone
→ Every reported hazard formally submitted to management within 48 hours
→ Termly campus safety audit submitted to management and Dean of Students
→ Every non-response after 21 days becomes public record
Pillar 03 — Infrastructure

Honest Pressure

Infrastructure requires government funding. I will not lie to you about that. What I will do is make the cost of inaction politically uncomfortable for every decision-maker.

→ Student-compiled infrastructure needs report submitted every term
→ Reports sent to management, HEA, and Ministry of Education
→ Every response and every silence published publicly
→ Make UNZA's funding gap a public conversation for media and government
Pillar 04 — Industrial Linkages

Honest Scope

I will not promise you a job pipeline in one year. I will promise you the foundation — verified, named, and published. Three real companies. Not MOUs. Commitments.

→ Audit every existing UNZA–industry MOU — active or ceremonial paper
→ Create a student skills register for final-year students
→ Secure 3 real companies committed to structured internship intakes
→ Names attached. Verified. Published.

The Accountability System

Three tools.
Day one.

01

The Monthly Public Report

Published every last Friday of the month. Distributed to every hall WhatsApp group. Posted on a dedicated public page. Printed on every hall noticeboard. Five things, without exception: what was raised, who was met and when, what response was received, what is unresolved and why, what happens next — with a deadline.

02

The Promise Tracker

Every commitment from this manifesto published publicly on day one of my term and updated every term. Three columns: Promise. Status. Evidence. Visible to every student for the entire duration of my presidency. No hiding. No rebranding failures as partial successes.

03

The Management Response Register

Every formal submission to management logged publicly with their response — or after 21 days, their documented silence. Published for every student, parent, journalist, and government official to see. No more invisible dangers. No more silence that costs lives.
